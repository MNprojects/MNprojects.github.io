iOS Application

For my final year project, I created an app for iOS mobile devices using Swift3 and Xcode. It was an educational app, designed to help children learn to spell in English while playing a game. The app mimiced some traditional techniques used for spelling while making use of the touch screen of the device. The user was able to create custom lists of words to test, as well as having some prebuilt lists that were suitable for different age groups. I made use of Apple's CoreData functionality to store the lists and words. The final product for the the university project lacked any real artwork or animation, making it unsuitable for release on the App Store.

Ice Shot!

A prototype of a small shooting game. The player is being chased by angry penguins with guns! As you try to move, you find that you slide on the ice until you hit a wall whereas the pengiuns will nimbly chase you in ever increasing numbers.

The prototype was built using Unity and C#. It was my first experience with either of these and took me about 3 days to create. There are still a lot of improvements to be made on this first iteration. The project is hosted on my GitHub page.
